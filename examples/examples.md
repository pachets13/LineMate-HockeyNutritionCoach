# Examples

How to read the examples in this folder.

The examples are split into two folders because they do two different jobs.

---

## `examples/situations/` — what good coaching looks like in one moment

Six files, each showing a different recurring coaching situation handled well. Each file is a single self-contained example with a different player, so the range of situations and player types is visible.

- **`01-onboarding.md`** — Tyler, second-year forward, mid-season, wants to clean up his day-to-day eating. Shows conversational onboarding (not a form), batched confirmations, capturing what the player wants from the coach.
- **`02-diagnostic-i-felt-slow.md`** — Jordan, first-year forward. Two variants of the same opener (*"I felt slow"*) with different underlying causes — one is a real nutrition issue, one isn't. Shows the nutrition-first diagnostic order and how the coach handles "this isn't a food problem."
- **`03-pregame-personalization.md`** — Connor, second-year defenseman. Three sessions showing the practice-day testing protocol unfold. Shows one-variable-at-a-time testing, two-session-minimum before moving to a game, and stopping when something works.
- **`04-billet-dynamics.md`** — Sam, first-year forward, billet with two younger kids in the house. The "what do I do about the billet's cooking?" conversation. Shows respecting the social reality and coaching the meals the player actually controls.
- **`05-fact-question-doorway.md`** — Ethan, second-year defenseman. Asks *"is creatine safe?"* Shows answering the fact first, naming the doorway underneath, and handing off honestly when the question is outside the coach's lane.
- **`06-schedule-handling.md`** — Zach, second-year centre. Drops a full-season PDF schedule mid-onboarding. Shows the coach reading the file, writing it into `schedule.md`, batching follow-up questions about travel times instead of asking eleven separate times, and pushing back honestly when the player's answers don't add up geographically.

Read these to see specific moves in action. Each file ends with a *"what this example demonstrates"* callout and (for most) a *"what a knowledge base would have said instead"* contrast.

---

## `examples/arc/` — what good coaching looks like over time

One file. One player. Five sessions.

- **`marcus-an-arc.md`** — Marcus, 17, first-year defenseman in the MJHL. From one week before training camp through about six weeks into the regular season. Shows how the coaching relationship builds, how the reference files get maintained over time, how accountability shows up across sessions, and how a player evolves from "nervous about feeding himself" to "comfortable with a working routine."

Read this to see how the methodology works as a relationship, not just a moment. The situation files show isolated good moves. The arc file shows them connected.

---

## How the two folders relate

If someone is trying to understand a specific coaching move, they should read the relevant situation file.

If someone is trying to understand how the whole folder operates as a system across multiple sessions — how files get maintained, how the coach holds continuity, how it actually feels to be coached by this over weeks — they should read the arc.

Both are useful. Neither replaces the other.
